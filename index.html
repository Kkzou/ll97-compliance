The model

XGBoost classifier with asymmetric thresholds

We trained a gradient-boosted decision tree ensemble on 1,593 anonymized buildings from the Building Data Genome Project 2. SMOTE oversampling balanced the minority "medium" class, and decision thresholds were tuned to prioritize catching high-risk buildings over raw accuracy — because missing a real high-risk warning is the costly error.

How it works

Two paths to your risk score.

The accuracy of your result depends on what data you can provide.

PATH A — WITH BILLS
Direct calculation
If you have 12 months of utility bills, we compute emissions exactly using LL97's official emission factors. You get exact tCO₂e values and penalty estimates.
PATH B — NO BILLS
ML classification
Without bills, our XGBoost classifier predicts your risk class from building features and climate. You get a class label and probability scores — but not exact emissions.
CLASSIFY
LL97 risk levels
High — above the cap. Medium — within 20% below. Low — more than 20% below. Classification follows LL97's official thresholds.
